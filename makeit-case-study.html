[ Overview ]

A campaign page designed for impact and speed.

The brief was simple: communicate a bold message and get people to act. I focused on making the hierarchy crystal clear — you should know exactly what this campaign is about within a few seconds of landing on the page.

I used Figma Make to get the structural layout down quickly, which was great for experimenting with flow and content placement. Then I brought it into Figma to actually design it — adding the visual craft and interaction details that Figma Make can't do on its own.

[ Process ]

Figma Make → Figma.

Step 1

Started in Figma Make

Starting in Figma Make meant I could try different structural approaches quickly without committing hours to a layout that might not work. Really useful at the ideation stage.

Step 2

Human Design in Figma

Once the structure felt right, I moved into Figma and made it mine — brand, typography, colour, interactions. This is the part where it goes from a skeleton to something that actually looks and feels good.
