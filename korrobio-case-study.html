[ Overview ]

Making cutting-edge science feel approachable.

KorroBio's work is genuinely complex — they're doing cutting-edge stuff at the intersection of biology and tech. The challenge was making that feel accessible and trustworthy to very different audiences: investors, researchers, and patients all needed to land on the same site and feel like it was made for them.

This was done before I introduced AI into my workflow — just Figma, research, and a lot of design thinking. I'm proud of it. It's also a useful benchmark: the same project today would take significantly less time.

[ Process ]

Pure Figma craft.

Research

UX Research & Strategy

I spent real time understanding how BioPharma companies communicate — what works, what doesn't, and how to make dense scientific content feel clear without dumbing it down.

Design

End-to-End in Figma

Everything was built in Figma — information architecture, visual design, data visualisation, the full component library, and a clean developer handoff. It was thorough, methodical work.

Reflection

Before AI — Full Craft Timeline

Looking back, this project took a lot longer than it needed to. The structural work is exactly what I now let AI handle. The difference in delivery time is significant. But the design itself? That would still look the same.
